📊 What are Brents?

Brents are critical modules that many other modules depend on. They represent potential bottlenecks or single points of failure in your codebase. High dependency on a single module means changes to that module could have widespread effects across your system.

📈 All 9 Metrics Explained

1️⃣ Fragility Score (0.0-1.0)
Range: 0=Low Risk (Green) to 1=High Risk (Red)
Overall brittleness risk score for the module. Formula: 0.35×(Incoming Dependencies) + 0.35×(Cycle Risk) + 0.20×(SCC Risk) + 0.10×(Betweenness Centrality).
Why it matters: Highest scoring modules are most likely to cause system failures when modified.
2️⃣ Incoming Dependencies (Count: 0-N)
How many modules depend on THIS module
The number of other modules that import/depend on this module. Higher values indicate this module is more critical.
Risk Levels: 0-2 (Low) | 3-10 (Medium) | 10+ (High - Core Dependency)
Why it matters: High incoming = changes here impact many downstream modules.
3️⃣ Outgoing Dependencies (Count: 0-N)
How many modules THIS module depends on
The number of other modules this module relies on. Higher values = tightly coupled to other components.
Risk Levels: 0-2 (Loosely Coupled) | 3-8 (Moderately Coupled) | 8+ (Tightly Coupled)
Why it matters: High outgoing = breaking changes in dependencies could break this module.
4️⃣ Degree Centrality (0.0-1.0)
Direct network importance
Measures how well-connected this module is (direct connections / total modules). Shows if it's a "hub" in the architecture.
Risk Levels: 0.0-0.2 (Peripheral) | 0.2-0.5 (Important Node) | 0.5+ (Critical Hub)
Why it matters: Identifies central modules that many others interact with directly.
5️⃣ Betweenness Centrality (0.0-1.0)
Bridge importance - shortest path broker
How often this module appears on the shortest dependency paths between other modules. High = module is a "bridge".
Risk Levels: 0.0-0.1 (Not a Bridge) | 0.1-0.3 (Minor Bridge) | 0.3+ (Critical Bridge)
Why it matters: Removing a high-betweenness module would disconnect many module pairs.
6️⃣ Closeness Centrality (0.0-1.0)
Network proximity - average distance to other modules
Average shortest path distance from this module to all other modules. High = close to most modules.
Risk Levels: 0.0-0.2 (Isolated/Peripheral) | 0.2-0.5 (Well-Positioned) | 0.5+ (Central Position)
Why it matters: Well-positioned modules have easier access to other parts of the system.
7️⃣ PageRank (0.0-1.0)
Network influence - importance propagation
Iterative importance score based on incoming links (like Google PageRank). High = many important modules depend on it.
Risk Levels: 0.0-0.01 (Minor Influence) | 0.01-0.03 (Moderate Influence) | 0.03+ (High Influence)
Why it matters: Shows which modules have highest network influence through dependencies.
8️⃣ Eigenvector Centrality (0.0-1.0)
Quality of connections - connected to important modules
Importance based on being connected to OTHER important modules (not just connection count). High = connected to high-quality nodes.
Risk Levels: 0.0-0.2 (Connected to Unimportant) | 0.2-0.5 (Connected to Moderate) | 0.5+ (Connected to Important)
Why it matters: Shows influence through proximity to other influential modules.
9️⃣ In Cycle / SCC Size
Circular dependency indicator & coupling region size
In Cycle: Yes ⚠️ = Part of circular dependency chain | No ✓ = No circular dependencies
SCC Size (Strongly Connected Component): Size of tightly-coupled region (1=isolated, 2-5=minor coupling, 5+=major hotspot)
Why it matters: Circular dependencies create unmaintainable "knots" - modules can't be changed independently.
